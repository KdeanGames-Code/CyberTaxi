CyberTaxi Gameplay Draft Document

Authors: Kevin-Dean

Version 1.1, July 24, 2025

Completed Chunks

These are the elements we've finalized so far. They form the foundation and can be adjusted post-playtest if needed.

1. Starting the Game

  • Website: Hosted at https://CyberTaxiGame.com. Default index.html is a store/signup page to attract users, show progress updates, and offer free/subscription options during development.
  • Free Tier: Basic access, same as subscription to start.
  • Subscription Tier: Provides additional in-game money ($140,000) to order 2 Model Ys and 2 RoboCabs (vehicles must be ordered, with delivery mechanics TBD). Money is added to the bank and can be used for any purchases (e.g., vehicles, garages, lots, SuperChargers).
  • Initial Bank Balance: $60,000, usable for any purchase at the player's discretion.

2. Vehicle Purchase Basics

  • Vehicle Options and Prices:
    • Tesla Model Y: $50,000 (based on 2025 pricing estimates).
    • Tesla RoboCab: $35,000 (estimated from Elon's posts; speculative).
  • Subscription Vehicles: Extra money ($140,000) allows ordering 2 Model Ys and 2 RoboCabs, but they require delivery (2-3 days if no stock at Tesla Service Centers, adjustable post-playtest).
  • Tesla Service Centers: New vehicles have daily inventory limits (3 to 8 vehicles per center; inventory arrives at random times, never consistent). Balance on playtesting.
  • Delivery Time: 2-3 days for new vehicles if no stock at Tesla Service Centers. Adjust after balance.
  • Used Market: Various wear levels (price reflects that, e.g., 10% base reduction from purchase price, further reduced by % of wear on the vehicle; repair before sale increases value). Limited number of used vehicles available.
  • Notifications: Notified when stock arrives (vehicles arrive only to garages, not lots). Notification in mobile as well.
  • Action: Player orders via the in-game browser (Tesla section), waits for delivery, and adds to garage/lot.

3. First-time Player Experience

  • Player-time Definition: 60 seconds in real life = 60 seconds in-game. Full real-time simulation—no accelerated time.

4. Earnings Mechanics Basics

  • How Earnings Work: Added to the bank when a CyberTaxi completes a ride (pickup to drop-off).
  • CyberTaxi is unavailable (not earning) during the trip—becomes available again after drop-off.
  • Rates: $4.20 base + $0.69/mile, adjustable post-playtest. Formula: Total Fare = $4.20 + ($0.69 × Distance in miles) - Tesla Share (percentage TBD).

5. Garage Setup Basics

  • Lease Terms: Monthly renewable, with options to pay quarterly (5% discount) or yearly (10% discount).
  • Locations: Available in various Austin areas with demand-based pricing (e.g., downtown high-demand = higher cost, suburbs low-demand = lower cost). Specific sites and numbers TBD (start with 5-10 locations, expand as users added).
  • Size and Cost: Small garage for 2 CyberTaxis, 500 sq ft, $1,500/month (adjustable post-playtest). Fits 2 cars (real-world 2-car garages are ~400-600 sq ft—500 sq ft is tight but doable with efficient layout). Lease/buy larger spaces if wanted, but costs more (e.g., means fewer revenue-generating vehicles).
  • Garages and Lots: Leases and outright purchase options, with selling capability. Selling returns 100% of the purchase price for base game (market value fluctuations TBD for version 2—no penalties).
  • Listings: Heat map toggle for demand (static for consistent day patterns, dynamic for time/events/special occasions).
  • Initial Garage State: Leased garage is empty. Options for plug-in charger (cheaper) vs. wireless (expensive). Construction time: Real-time-ish (e.g., 1-3 days for chargers, depending on number; week or two for expansions).
  • Construction Bars: Progress bars for construction (e.g., charger install), with map toggle for construction sites and demand views. Design as "next-level Space X shit" (futuristic, neon visuals).
  • Charging Rates: Cheaper than SuperChargers (e.g., $0.12/kWh vs. $0.25-$0.50/kWh public). Adjust post-playtest.
  • Action: Player selects and pays for a garage from a list or map after signup; it becomes their base.
  • Parking Spaces: Separate from vehicle slots (e.g., 3 parking spaces for 500 sq ft garage, giving total 5 spaces—more cars require lots or new locations; future expansions).
  • Perks: No explicit perks (e.g., +20% earnings); proximity to demand is the natural perk (closer garages allow faster response to fares, as closest free CyberTaxi gets the ride).

6. License System

  • License Requirement: All CyberTaxis need an Austin operating license ($1,000/year per vehicle, renewable; penalties for lapses TBD).
  • Free License: City of Austin grants a Taxi permit for max 10 vehicles. Vehicles still cost in-game money.
  • Subscriber License: Called "Taxi License," provides unlimited vehicles. However, a taxi permit is needed for each vehicle at a set rate per year (in-game money, paid to the "game city").
  • Action: Auto-applied on purchase/delivery; reminders for renewal.

7. SuperCharger Setup

  • Installation Costs and Power Requirements: Home Charger (Wall Connector): $1,700 total (~$500 unit + $1,200 install, 11.5 kW / 44 miles/hour), V2 SuperCharger: $50,000 install (150 kW, 30-60 min for 0-80%), V3 SuperCharger: $75,000 install (250 kW, 20-30 min for 0-80%), V4 SuperCharger: $100,000 install (325 kW, 15-25 min for 0-80%), Wireless V2 deferred to version 2 (~$2,000+, ~15 kW, 1-2 hours for full charge).
  • Construction Times: Home: 1 day, V2: 3 days, V3: 5 days, V4: 7 days.
  • Garage/Lot Integration: SuperChargers installed in garages or lots (cost $’s, take time), garage size limits types (e.g., 500 sq ft caps at V2).
  • Locations: Tied to real Tesla SuperCharger sites in Austin (6 initial).
  • Manual Charging Action: Vehicles notify on arrival, player clicks to charge (80/100% set), disconnect early pays for power used.
  • Notifications: Arrival, start, complete with timer.
  • Per-Charge Costs: $0.12/kWh garage/lot, $0.25-$0.50/kWh SuperCharger.
  • Charge Progress Monitoring: Battery bar with hover %, real-time updates every 5 min.
  • Cost Deduction: Prorated on power used.
  • Mobile Integration: Notifications/timer, defer UI to Front End Dev.

8. Vehicle Wear and Tires

  • Wear Mechanics: Increases with use/conditions (e.g., 0.1% per mile, based on Tesla data; battery degradation over time).
  • Wear Increases: 0.001% per mile, 5-10% degradation after 100,000 miles, +20% in traffic/protests, +10% in weather.
  • Tires: Seasonal options (e.g., snow tires for winter events, $500/set).
  • Tires: Seasonal options (snow for v2), wear faster in harsh conditions, last 30,000-50,000 miles, rotate every 6,250 miles.
  • Maintenance Costs: Annual $400-$600, tire replacement $800-$1,200, 5-year $2,000-$3,000.
  • Battery Replacement: $10,000-$20,000 at 20-30% degradation.
  • Action: Monitor via Vehicle Details, high wear triggers maintenance (mechanics TBD).

9. Charging Costs

  • Rates: $0.12/kWh at garage/home, $0.25-$0.50/kWh at SuperCharger.
  • Power Usage: 0.25-0.3 kWh/mile for Model Y.

10. Staff for Repairs/Cleaning

  • Hiring Costs and Roles: Mechanic $4,000/month (reduces costs to $200-$400/year vs. $400-$600 service center, tire rotation $100), Cleaning Staff $2,500/month (auto-cleans during shifts).
  • Impact: Mechanics cut costs/time by 50%.
  • Staffing Hours: X hours/day (8-12 configurable), player schedules shifts.
  • Cleaning: Auto with staff, manual button without (no earning during garage).
  • Action: Hire via in-game browser, pay monthly, staff levels up.

11. User Profiles

  • Basic Fields: Username, email, bank balance.
  • Advanced Fields: Fleet overview, progress tracking, garages (list with sizes, locations, chargers).
  • Company Profile: Company Name, overview with expandable details (e.g., click V3 chargers for list/details). "Company" flyout button.
  • Action: Access/update via in-game browser.

12. Environmental Factors and Events

  • Weather/Traffic: Affects routes/earnings (e.g., rain slows trips, protests block areas).
  • Weather: Rain in Austin (10-20% chance/day, -10-20% speed, +10% wear), +20% demand, snow/winter tires deferred to v2.
  • Traffic: Rush hour peaks (7-9 AM/4-6 PM, +15% route time, +5% wear).
  • Protests: 5% chance/week, blocks 1-2 hours, +20% wear if caught, bonus is evasion.
  • Integration: Open-Meteo API (worldwide, free, no key), 15-min queries with caching, CyberTaxi browser "Weather Forecast" section.
  • Action: Notifications (e.g., "Rain in Downtown—reroute?"), player responds.

13. Demand and Leaderboards

  • Zones: High/low demand on map (dynamic based on time/events).
  • Demand: High/low demand zones on heat map toggle, with static patterns (e.g., downtown high day) and dynamic events (e.g., +20% from weather). Nightlife zones (bars 8 PM-2 AM, concerts/city events based on real Austin schedules, +30-50% demand) boost fares. Closest free CyberTaxi gets the ride.
  • Leaderboards: Track revenue, distance, and nightlife fares (e.g., badges for top night earners), daily/weekly rankings.
  • Action: Player places vehicles in high-demand/nightlife zones, leaderboards update real-time in Company Profile.

14. Tutorials and Onboarding

  • First-Time Setup: Player signs up via store page, receives $60,000 bank, chooses a demand-based garage from map/list, leases it, and orders a first vehicle from the Tesla section (2-3 days delivery if no stock).
  • Action: Step-by-step prompts trigger on first login (e.g., "Welcome to CyberTaxi! Let’s get your fleet rolling—lease a garage here."), with map highlights, "Next"/"Skip Tutorial" options, and auto-saved progress via localStorage. Notifications for delivery, auto-adding to garage.
  • In-Game Guides: Pop-ups/tool tips for mechanics (e.g., "Hover battery bar for %—charge at 80% for faster turns"), interactive fare tips (e.g., "Closest taxi gets the ride—place in high-demand zones").
  • Action: Guides trigger on first use (e.g., garage lease tutorial, wear alert), skippable via a persistent "Tutorial Off" toggle or "Restart Tutorial" in User Profiles.
  • Chunk Status: Complete—no open questions remain.
