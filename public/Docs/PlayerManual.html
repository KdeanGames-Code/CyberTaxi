CyberTaxi Player Manual (Detailed Draft)

This detailed draft of the CyberTaxi Player Manual is based on the latest Game Design Document (GDD), Gameplay Draft, and Project Plan as of July 29, 2025. It expands on core mechanics, UI interactions, strategies, and troubleshooting for testing purposes. All mechanics align with Completed Chunks 1-14, with notes on pending features. Use this for onboarding simulations and balance feedback.

Overview

CyberTaxi is a strategy/simulation game where you manage a fleet of autonomous Tesla RoboTaxis in real-time urban environments, starting in Austin, TX. As a Progressive Web App (PWA), it supports offline play for viewing cached data (e.g., fleet status, maps) and syncs upon reconnection. The game targets strategy fans, Tesla enthusiasts, and gamers who enjoy deep management sims.

Core Loop: Purchase/lease vehicles and garages, deploy your fleet to high-demand areas, earn fares from rides, manage wear/battery/maintenance, and expand operations while competing on leaderboards. Time runs in full real-time (1:1 ratio—no acceleration), encouraging ongoing engagement via notifications.

Key Themes: Realism in autonomous taxi management, competition for fares, and adaptation to urban challenges like demand fluctuations and events.

Platform Notes: Access via https://CyberTaxiGame.com. Install as a PWA for home screen addition, offline access, and push notifications (e.g., vehicle deliveries, low battery alerts).

Tip: For optimal experience, enable notifications during signup to stay alerted on real-time events like stock arrivals or maintenance completions, even when the app is closed.
Warning: Offline mode limits actions like purchases or live routing—reconnect for full functionality. Data syncs automatically to prevent loss.

1. Starting the Game

Begin at https://CyberTaxiGame.com, where the landing page serves as a store/signup hub. It showcases progress updates, gameplay teasers, and tier options to hook new players.

  • Signup Process: Enter email/username/password. Skippable onboarding modals guide you through basics (e.g., "Welcome! Let's lease your first garage."). Modals include tooltips and progress trackers.
  • Free Tier: Starts with $60,000 bank balance. Use it freely for any purchase, like a small garage or your first vehicle.
  • Subscription Tier: Adds $140,000 to your bank, enabling faster scaling (e.g., ordering 2 Model Ys and 2 RoboCabs). This money is versatile—spend on garages, chargers, or staff. Subscriptions support development and unlock cosmetic badges on leaderboards.
  • Initial Bank Balance: $60,000 (free) or $200,000 total (subscription). Displayed in the top menu with real-time updates.

UI Entry Point: Post-signup, load into the Austin map view with top menu (bank, score, energy bar) and side buttons for flyouts (fleet, garage, company, charging).

Advanced Strategy: Use initial funds to lease a suburban garage (low cost) for storage, then expand downtown for high-demand fares. Monitor the demand heat map toggle early.
Warning: Bank overdrafts aren't possible—purchases fail if funds are insufficient, prompting a "Insufficient Funds" modal with earning tips.

Access the in-game Tesla browser section via the fleet flyout or map menu. Vehicles are essential for earning—order new or browse used markets.

Pro Tip: Time purchases around demand surges (e.g., events)—monitor service centers via map pins for stock. Buy used for quick starts, but factor in repair costs (e.g., 5-15% of price based on wear).
Warning: Vehicles without garage space auto-park at public lots (higher fees)—always expand storage proactively.

3. First-Time Player Experience

New players receive guided onboarding to ease into real-time mechanics. The game emphasizes frustration-free entry with skippable elements.

  • Time Mechanics: Full real-time: 60 real seconds = 60 in-game seconds. No fast-forward—plan for session-based play (e.g., check-ins for management).
  • Onboarding Flow: Post-signup modals: 1. Welcome overview. 2. Garage selection map (heat map tutorial). 3. First vehicle order demo. 4. Basic controls (flyouts, map toggles). Restart option in settings.
  • Tutorials: Contextual tooltips (e.g., hover on energy bar: "Tracks fleet battery—charge to avoid downtime."). Offline review via cached modals.
Efficiency Hack: Skip onboarding if experienced, but revisit for advanced tips like wear formulas in the details flyout.
Warning: Real-time means delays (e.g., deliveries) persist across sessions—use notifications to avoid missing key moments.

4. Earnings Mechanics

Earnings drive progression—complete rides to add fares to your bank. View history in vehicle details or company profile.

Verbatim Formula (from GDD): Total Fare = $4.20 (base) + $0.69/mile - Tesla Share (percentage TBD, adjustable post-playtest).

  • How It Works: Vehicles pick up rides autonomously in demand zones. Unavailable during trips (real-time duration based on distance/traffic). Earnings credited on drop-off.
  • Fare History: Collapsible table in details window: Date, Distance, Fare, Deductions. Example: Short downtown ride = $10-15; Airport haul = $50+.
  • Leaderboards: Global rankings by total earnings, efficiency (fares/mile), fleet size. Daily/weekly resets with badges.
Example Calculation: Distance = 10 miles → Fare = 4.20 + (0.69 * 10) = 11.10 - Tesla Share (e.g., 20% = 8.88 net).
Optimization Strategy: Deploy in high-demand (heat map red zones) for frequent short rides. Rotate vehicles to minimize downtime—aim for 80%+ utilization.
Warning: Long trips tie up vehicles longer—balance with demand; ignored low battery reduces fares by 10-20% due to inefficiency.

5. Garage Setup

Garages are hubs for storage, charging, and maintenance. Lease/buy via map listings with demand-based pricing.

  • Lease Terms (Verbatim): Monthly ($1,500 for small 500 sq ft, 2-vehicle garage), quarterly (5% discount), yearly (10% discount). Renewable auto unless canceled.
  • Locations & Costs: Downtown (high demand, premium price); Suburbs (low cost, slower fares). 5-10 initial sites, expandable. Heat map toggle shows static (daily patterns) and dynamic (events) demand.
  • Sizes: Small (2 vehicles, tight fit); Larger options cost more but hold more (e.g., 5+ spots). Parking slots separate from charging.
  • Purchase/Sell: Buy for permanence (higher upfront); Sell returns 100% (no fluctuations in v1).
  • Initial State & Upgrades: Empty on lease. Add chargers (plug-in cheap/slow vs. wireless expensive/fast). Construction: 1-3 days real-time with progress bars.
  • Action Steps: 1. Toggle garage map overlay. 2. Select site (info popup: cost, demand). 3. Confirm lease/buy. 4. Monitor construction timer.
Strategic Insight: Start suburban for affordability, upgrade chargers early (e.g., wireless reduces downtime by 30%). Use offline mode to plan layouts via cached maps.
Warning: Overcrowded garages incur fees—expand before fleet growth. Construction halts operations in that garage.

6. Licenses and Permits

Required for legal operations—obtain via in-game city hall browser section.

  • Types: Per-location permits (cost scales with zone demand, e.g., $500 downtown). Annual renewals with fleet-size fees.
  • Application: Form flyout with approval timer (1-2 days real-time).
  • Compliance: Track in company profile—expirations trigger warnings.
Budget Tip: Bundle renewals yearly for discounts; prioritize high-demand zones first.
Warning: Operating without permits risks fines (10-20% of daily earnings) or vehicle impounds (downtime until resolved).

7. Chargers and Energy Management

Install in garages for recharging—critical for uptime.

  • Types: Plug-in (affordable, slower rate: ~50% charge/hour); Wireless (premium, faster: ~80% charge/hour). Costs: $500-2,000 each.
  • Energy Bar (UI): Top menu fleet-wide tracker (hover: percentage, tips). Low levels flash warnings.
  • Charging Rates: Garage: $0.12/kWh; Public SuperChargers: $0.25-0.50/kWh (slower for non-Tesla spots).
  • Offline Support: Cached battery/wear data for monitoring; sync charges on reconnect.
  • Action: In garage flyout, select install → choose type → confirm (deducts bank, starts timer).
Pro Hack: Schedule auto-charging during low-demand nights via settings toggle—maintain 70%+ average for max efficiency.
Warning: Ignoring low battery causes breakdowns (towing fees: $100-500) and lost fares.

8. Vehicle Wear and Maintenance

Wear accumulates with usage—monitor to avoid breakdowns.

  • Wear Formula (Verbatim Suggestion): Increases per mile (e.g., 0.1% base + modifiers for traffic/weather). Visible in badges: Green (0-30%), Yellow (31-60%), Red (61-100%).
  • Maintenance: Schedule in garage (costs 5-15% of vehicle price based on wear; time: 1-4 hours real-time).
  • Repairs: For 70%+ wear—downtime 1-2 days, higher costs.
  • UI: Progress bars in fleet list; alerts for thresholds.
Preventive Strategy: Rotate high-wear vehicles to garage early; hire mechanics to reduce times by 20-50%.
Warning: 100% wear triggers auto-repair (forced downtime, double costs)—proactive checks via details flyout.

9. Staff Hiring

Hire for garage efficiency—access via company flyout.

  • Roles: Mechanics (reduce maintenance time/costs); Cleaners (boost vehicle appeal for +5-10% fares).
  • Costs: Salaries $500-1,500/month based on experience (profiles show stats: e.g., "Expert Mechanic: -30% repair time").
  • Hiring Process: Browse listings → Select → Pay signing bonus → Assign to garage.
Team Building: Start with one mechanic per garage; scale with fleet size for ROI (e.g., saves 10-20% on long-term costs).
Warning: Staff turnover if underpaid—monitor morale in profiles (low = quit risk).

10. Company Profiles

Customize and track progress via side flyout.

  • Overview: Fleet/garage stats, total earnings, efficiency metrics. Editable: Name, logo (upload or presets).
  • Dashboard: Progress trackers (e.g., "Fleet Utilization: 75%"), fare summaries.
  • Leaderboards Integration: View your rank; compare anonymized competitors.
Branding Tip: Unique profiles attract in-game partnerships (pending events)—focus on efficiency for top ranks.

11. Environmental Events

Dynamic challenges affect operations—toggle overlays on map.

  • Types: Weather (rain slows speed -20%), Traffic (delays rides), Protests (blocks zones).
  • Impacts: Reduced fares/speed; alerts via notifications (e.g., "Rain in Downtown: Adapt routes!").
  • Forecast: 24-hour preview in weather flyout.
Adaptation Strategy: Diversify garages across zones; use events for boosts (e.g., post-event surges +20% demand).
Warning: Unprepared events can halt fleets—monitor forecasts daily.

12. Demand and Routing

Demand drives fares—use heat maps for placement.

  • Heat Maps: Static (e.g., downtown peaks evenings); Dynamic (events spike +20-50%). Toggle via map menu.
  • Routing: Autonomous via API (e.g., OpenRouteService). Click vehicle marker for path overlay (blue polyline).
  • Closest Taxi: System assigns rides to nearest available vehicle.
Demand Mastery: Position idle vehicles in red zones; combine with staff for +fares during surges.

13. Leaderboards and Competition

Compete globally—access via profile flyout.

  • Rankings: Earnings, Efficiency (fares/vehicle), Fleet Size. Badges for top 10%.
  • Resets: Daily/Weekly for fresh challenges; historical views.
  • Profiles: View competitors (anonymized stats) for benchmarking.
Competitive Edge: Focus on efficiency over size—low wear/high utilization scores higher.

14. Tutorials and Onboarding

Ensures accessibility—integrated throughout.

  • Modals: Skippable, step-by-step (e.g., "Click here to order your first vehicle").
  • Tooltips/Alerts: Contextual (e.g., low energy: "Charge now!"). Restart in settings.
  • Notifications: Deliveries, events, thresholds (push for mobile).
Learning Curve: Use offline cached tutorials for review; advanced sections unlock post-milestones (e.g., after first fare).

UI and Controls Overview

Map: Leaflet-based dark mode with vehicle markers (gold: active, red: parked/garage, white: new). Click for popups/routes.

Windows/Flyouts: Draggable (fleet lists with badges: ID, wear/battery bars, status). Details: Image, history table, maintenance button.

Top Menu: Bank ($ icon), Score, Energy bar (fill: blue, hover tips).

Side Buttons: Circular flyouts (fleet options: active/parked/garage/new). Hover titles for guidance.

Offline: Cached views (maps, stats); actions queue for sync.

UX Pro Tip: Customize shortcuts (pending settings); use resize on windows for multi-view management.

Troubleshooting and Advanced Tips

  • Low Earnings: Check demand zones, battery/wear, permits. Test: Simulate rides via dev console (if enabled).
  • Offline Issues: Ensure PWA installed; purchases require online—queued alerts on reconnect.
  • Balance Feedback: Report via in-game form (e.g., "Fares too low?"). Testing: Focus on real-time feel.
  • Meta Strategies: Community forums (future) for tips; diversify for events; aim for 500+ score via efficiency.
